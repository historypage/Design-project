William of Malmesbury

William of Malmesbury was an English historian who lived from 1095 to 1143. He is considered to be one of the best English historians after Bede the Venerable (died 735). His main work consisted of two novels: Gesta Regum Anglorum and Gesta Pontificum Anglorum. In these two novels, William of Malmesbury documented the history of the English and this historian's writing style and documentation skills have made him popular among other historians.
